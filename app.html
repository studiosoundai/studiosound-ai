Heads up: converting MP3 to WAV meets distributor format requirements, but it can't restore quality lost in the original MP3. For best results, always export WAV directly from your recording session when you can.
Why WAV?
The 10-second version
DISTRIBUTORS REQUIRE IT
Most distributors only accept WAV (16-bit or 24-bit, 44.1kHz). MP3 uploads get rejected or re-encoded badly.
WHAT THIS TOOL OUTPUTS
Standard 16-bit / 44.1kHz stereo WAV — the exact format DSPs expect for distribution.
